ASSEMBLY, No. 2608

 

STATE OF NEW JERSEY

 

209th LEGISLATURE

 

INTRODUCED JUNE 19, 2000

 

 

Sponsored by:

Assemblyman LEROY J. JONES, JR.

District 27 (Essex)

 

 

 

 

SYNOPSIS

    Requires high school seniors to complete a program of community service as a condition of graduation.

 

CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT

    As introduced.

 


An Act concerning community service by high school students and supplementing chapter 35 of Title 18A of the New Jersey Statutes.

 

    Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey:

 

    1. The Legislature finds and declares that:

    a. a person is not only an individual, but part of a broader community of family, friends, neighbors and fellow citizens;

    b. an important goal of public education should be to teach young people about their role in society and to develop in them a sense of civic responsibility, especially toward those in the community who may demonstrate greater social need, such as the elderly, the sick, the poor, and the homeless;

    c. a student can best learn about civic responsibility by participating in community organizations and activities which are aimed at addressing some of the social problems and concerns facing our society;

    d. a number of school districts throughout the country currently require high school students to participate in a community service program, and in fact, the Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching, in its report "High School," recommended establishing a mandatory community service requirement for high school students as a condition of graduation; and

    e. the establishment of a community service requirement for high school students in this State would provide young people with an opportunity to actively participate in their communities and would help them to mature into responsible citizens and adults with strong social and civic values.

 

    2. Beginning with the 12th grade class in the 2001-2002 school year and including every 12th grade class thereafter, each high school senior shall complete a program of 15 hours of community service as a condition of graduation from high school. A board of education may exempt a student from all or part of the required service, however, if, in the judgement of the board, the requirement would result in undue hardship for the student. In addition, a board of education may exempt a student who is classified pursuant to chapter 46 of Title 18A of the New Jersey Statutes from all or part of the service required by this act if, in the judgment of the board, the performance of that service would be inconsistent with the Individualized Education Plan (IEP) of that student.

 

    3. Each local board of education shall, pursuant to regulations promulgated by the State Board of Education, adopt a program of community service for high school seniors. The program shall provide that:

    a. a student shall complete a minimum of 15 hours of community service during the student's senior year in high school;

    b. a student may complete the community service requirement during the evenings, on weekends, or during the summer, but shall not complete the requirement during school hours;

    c. a student may complete the community service requirement with a non-profit agency or organization, a public agency or institution, a non-profit or for-profit health care facility, or any other community organization which the board deems appropriate; and

    d. a student shall not receive compensation for community service or substitute employment for the service requirement.

    The Department of Education shall, at the request of a local board of education, provide any technical assistance which may be necessary to aid a district in the planning and implementation of its community service program.

 

    4. The Commissioner of Education shall periodically review the progress of the community service program to ensure that the program is achieving the intended goals, and shall report to the Governor and the Legislature on the findings of the review, including any recommendations for changes which are deemed appropriate.

 

    5. Nothing in this act shall be construed to exempt the agencies, organizations, and institutions which permit high school students to perform community services from complying with child labor laws pursuant to P.L.1940, c.153 (C.34:2-21.1 et seq.).

 

    6. The State Board of Education shall adopt rules and regulations pursuant to the "Administrative Procedure Act," P.L.1968, c.410 (C.52:14B-1 et seq.) necessary to effectuate the purposes of this act.

 

    7. This act shall take effect immediately.

 

 

STATEMENT

 

    This bill requires local school boards to adopt a community service program for high school seniors that would begin in the 2001-2002 school year.

    Under the provisions of the bill a student would be required to complete a minimum of 15 hours of community service during the student's senior year as a condition of graduation. A student could complete the community service requirement during evenings, on weekends, or during the summer, but not during school hours. A student may work with a non-profit or public agency or organization, a non-profit or for-profit health care facility, or some other community organization which the local school board deems appropriate. However, a student could not receive compensation for the hours spent completing the community service requirement. The bill provides that the agencies, organizations, and institutions which permit high school students to perform community services would not be exempt from child labor laws.

    The bill permits a board of education to excuse a student from all or part of the requirement if the board determines that the service requirement would pose an undue hardship for the student. In addition, a special education pupil could be excused if the board determines that the service requirement is inconsistent with the pupil's Individualized Education Plan (IEP).
